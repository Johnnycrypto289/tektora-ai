# Advice Call Guide — 15-Minute Strategy Session

> This is the actual strategy call that was booked via Atlas. Run by Boss or AI. The goal: understand their stack, quantify their pain, and transition into a proposal if there's a fit.

---

## Before the Call

- [ ] Review their contact info from Atlas call notes
- [ ] Look up their business (Google, website, reviews)
- [ ] Have a blank proposal template ready
- [ ] Have a calculator open for quick math
- [ ] Know their name, business name, and any tools they mentioned

---

## Call Structure

| Segment | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Rapport & Discovery | 5 min | Learn about their business |
| Stack Audit | 5 min | Document every tool they pay for |
| Pain & Frustration | 3 min | Find the emotional trigger |
| Transition | 2 min | Plant the seed or close |

---

## Segment 1: Rapport & Discovery (0:00 - 5:00)

### Open Warm

> "Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time. I know 15 minutes isn't a lot so I'll keep this tight. Before we dive in, tell me a little about your business."

### Questions to Ask

1. **"What kind of work do you guys do?"**
   - Plumbing, HVAC, electrical, roofing, general contracting, landscaping, etc.

2. **"How big is your crew?"**
   - Solo? 2-5? 5-15? 15+?

3. **"Roughly how much revenue are you doing monthly?"**
   - Don't push if they're uncomfortable. Offer ranges:
   > "Are you guys doing under $50k/month, $50-$100k, or north of that?"

4. **"How long have you been in business?"**

5. **"What's your biggest challenge right now — is it getting more work or managing the work you've got?"**

### What You're Listening For

- Size of business = size of opportunity
- Revenue range = budget indicator
- "Managing the work" = they need tools (our sweet spot)
- "Getting more work" = they need marketing (we can help here too)

---

## Segment 2: Stack Audit (5:00 - 10:00)

### The Key Question

> "Cool. Now let me ask you this — what software or tools are you paying for monthly to run the business? Walk me through your stack."

### Prompt If They Stall

> "So like, what do you use for scheduling? Invoicing? Estimates? Do you use anything for managing your guys in the field?"

### Common Tools to Listen For

| Category | Common Tools | Typical Monthly Cost |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Field Service Management | Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan | $50 - $500/mo |
| Accounting | QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Xero | $30 - $200/mo |
| CRM | GoHighLevel, HubSpot, Salesforce | $50 - $300/mo |
| Website | Squarespace, Wix, WordPress hosting | $15 - $50/mo |
| Reviews | Birdeye, Podium, NiceJob | $100 - $400/mo |
| Answering Service | Ruby, Smith.ai, AnswerConnect | $100 - $500/mo |
| Marketing/Ads | LSA, Google Ads, Thumbtack | $200 - $2,000/mo |
| Communication | Slack, Teams, Google Workspace | $10 - $30/mo |
| Payments | Square, Stripe fees | Variable |
| Proposals | Joist, CompanyCam, Estimate Rocket | $20 - $100/mo |

### Document Everything

As they talk, fill in this table:

| Tool | What It Does | Monthly Cost | Happy With It? |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| | | | |
| | | | |
| | | | |
| | | | |
| | | | |
| **TOTAL** | | **$______/mo** | |

### Do the Math Out Loud

> "Okay, so let me add this up... You've got [Tool A] at $X, [Tool B] at $Y, [Tool C] at $Z... that's about $[Total] a month. That's $[Total x 12] a year. Does that sound right?"

*Let them react. This number usually surprises them.*

---

## Segment 3: Pain & Frustration (10:00 - 13:00)

### The Emotional Question

> "What frustrates you most about these tools? If you could wave a magic wand, what would you change?"

### Follow-Up Probes

- **"Do any of these tools NOT talk to each other?"** (Integration pain)
- **"Have you ever wanted to cancel one of these but felt stuck?"** (Lock-in pain)
- **"If one of these raised their price tomorrow, what would you do?"** (Dependency pain)
- **"Do you feel like you're actually using all the features you're paying for?"** (Waste pain)

### What You're Listening For

- Frustration with cost = price-motivated buyer
- Frustration with complexity = simplicity-motivated buyer
- Frustration with lock-in = ownership-motivated buyer
- Frustration with support = service-motivated buyer

### Validate Their Pain

> "Yeah, I hear that a lot. You're basically renting your business tools and if you ever stop paying, you lose everything — your client data, your history, all of it."

---

## Segment 4: The Transition (13:00 - 15:00)

### The Bridge

> "Interesting — so here's what we do, and this is why I wanted to talk to you. We actually build custom AI tools that replace all of that. You pay once, you own everything. No monthly fees. No vendor lock-in. The code is yours."

### Pause. Let it land.

> "Would you like to see what that would look like for your business?"

---

### If YES:

> "Awesome. Here's what I'm going to do — I'll put together a custom proposal based on everything you just told me. It'll show your current costs vs. what we'd build, the total savings, and exactly what you'd get. I'll have it to you within 48 hours. Sound good?"

**Actions:**
1. Confirm their email for the proposal
2. Set expectation: "I'll send it over by [Day]. Let's book a 10-minute call to walk through it together."
3. Send booking link for proposal review call
4. Build proposal using proposal-template.md

---

### If MAYBE / NOT SURE:

> "Totally fair. How about this — let me put together a quick comparison showing what you're spending now vs. what it would look like to own everything. No obligation, just so you can see the math. Can I send that over?"

*Most people say yes to this. It's low commitment.*

**Actions:**
1. Send a simplified version of the proposal (just the math section)
2. Follow up in 3 days

---

### If NO:

> "No problem at all. I appreciate you taking the time. Can I follow up in 2 weeks? Sometimes this stuff marrinates and makes more sense down the road."

**Actions:**
1. Add to 2-week follow-up sequence
2. Log notes on why they said no
3. Send a thank-you text: "Thanks for the chat, [Name]. If anything changes, you've got my number."

---

## Post-Call Checklist

- [ ] Log all notes in CRM
- [ ] Calculate their total monthly/annual software spend
- [ ] Identify which tools we'd replace and with what
- [ ] Determine proposal tier (Starter / Growth / Full Stack)
- [ ] Send proposal within 48 hours (or simplified math if "maybe")
- [ ] Book proposal review call
- [ ] Add to appropriate follow-up sequence
- [ ] Update lead status: Hot / Warm / Cold

---

## Key Principles

1. **Listen more than you talk.** This is about THEM, not you.
2. **Never badmouth their current tools.** Just highlight the cost and the model.
3. **Let the math do the selling.** When they see $12,000/year vs. a one-time $5,000 build, it sells itself.
4. **Don't push.** If they're not ready, respect it. The follow-up sequence does the work.
5. **Always end with a next step.** Never hang up without a clear next action.
